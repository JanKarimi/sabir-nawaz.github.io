Who am I?

My name is Sabir Nawaz from khost Afghanistan.Born on 29/12/1996, and have bachelor degree from BBA faculty. In 2008, I was deprived of my father's shadow while the responsibility of the whole family was on me. Despite the many hardships and problems in life, I have always tried to play my human responsibility for a happy and peaceful world, So in the 2016 year, I invented academic centers in different areas of Khost to teach girls and boys and show them a positive life, so that Afghans can understand the value of human beings, life and time like other international people.

That the world has an example of a common house and a common society so In the same way, all international forces should have a positive life motive for the futureØŒ And everyone should try to make this common house happy. There are many poor countries in the world, one of which is Afghanistan, whose people have always sacrificed against terrorism. The world (common house) that have the right on us to keep it safe and beautiful so as a humanitarian person am ready to sacrifice my life and my financial incomes so that we can eliminate all the upcoming problems that Afghans and international faces in the future.

Our Programs

EDUCATION SUPPORT

Provide teaching and learning materials for schools and students, provide standard education to people in formal and non-formal schools, such as: community-based education and accelerated education Programs for children who have been out of school for years. Lack of access to schools and working to improve the poor quality of existing schools. Afghan Rescue Charity Foundation provides standard and professional education opportunities to children, youth and adults help them to learn the skills they need to survive and thrive.

HEALTY WATER

Although Afghanistan has sufficient reserves of safe drinking water which can be exported to other countries, unfortunately due to misuse of this water we are facing shortage of drinking water in the country. According to a study by the International Action Aid Institute in the United Kingdom, Afghanistan is a country where seventy (70) out of every hundred (100) people do not have access to safe drinking water.

RAISING ORPHANS

The fact that our country has been plagued by the terrible disease of war for decades. As a result, many of our women widows and children have been orphaned, so the Afghan Rescue Charity Foundation wants to create special programs for the care of widows and orphans. Construction of orphanage. Provide educational opportunities for orphans. Provide financial and spiritual assistance to other orphanages. Provide employment opportunities for widows and orphans. Provide incentive programs for orphans and homeless children.

ECONOMIC

Currently Afghanistan is a fully reliance on aid country. We want to have a country that has its own resources and its own stable economy where people don't need others to help. Afghan Recue Charity Foundation works to increases livelihood opportunities for long-term economic well-being. The Afghan Rescue Charity Foundation strives to provide health services, safe water, shelter, employment opportunities and basic household necessities to people in remote areas.
